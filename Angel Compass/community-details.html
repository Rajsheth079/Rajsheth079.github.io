Therapy

Therapy (also called counseling) is a process where a trained mental health professional helps individuals or groups address psychological, emotional issues.

1. Common Types of Therapy

Therapy Type Description Common Uses
Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) Focuses on changing unhelpful thoughts and behaviors. Anxiety, depression, OCD, phobias
Psychodynamic Therapy Explores unconscious patterns rooted in childhood. Deep-rooted emotional issues
Humanistic Therapy Emphasizes personal growth and self-actualization. Low self-esteem, life dissatisfaction
Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT) Includes emotion regulation and mindfulness strategies. Borderline personality disorder, intense emotions
EMDR Uses eye movements to process traumatic memories. PTSD, trauma recovery
Family/Couples Therapy Focuses on communication and relationship dynamics. Family conflict, relationship issues
Group Therapy One or more therapists working with a group of people. Addiction recovery, support, social skills

2. Therapy Process: Step by Step

  • 1. Initial Consultation: Meet the therapist, discuss goals, and determine fit.
  • 2. Assessment: Explore background, symptoms, and emotional patterns.
  • 3. Goal Setting: Define clear focus areas for therapy outcomes.
  • 4. Regular Sessions: Weekly discussions, tools, and reflection.
  • 5. Review & Completion: Evaluate progress and decide on continuation or closure.

3. Goals of Therapy

  • Improve mental health and emotional regulation
  • Gain insight into thoughts, feelings, and behavior
  • Develop coping skills and build resilience
  • Resolve trauma or grief
  • Strengthen relationships and communication
  • Improve self-esteem and decision-making
